This organization follows a clear and fair promotion process to ensure that employees are advanced based on performance, behavior, and readiness for higher responsibility. Promotions are not automatic and are granted only when all required conditions are met.

Mandatory Promotion Criteria

For an employee to be considered for promotion, all of the following criteria must be satisfied:

Performance Results
The employee must consistently meet or exceed their assigned goals over at least two review periods. One exceptional quarter alone is not sufficient for promotion.

Quality of Work and Responsibility
The employee must show the ability to handle responsibilities equal to or greater than the next role, including decision-making, ownership of tasks, and reliability.

Behavior and Team Impact
Feedback from peers and managers must indicate professional conduct, cooperation, communication, and respect for others. Strong results achieved through harmful behavior are not acceptable.

Role Readiness
The employee should demonstrate readiness to take on broader scope, guide others when needed, and manage increased expectations without close supervision.

Handling Contradictions in Reviews

If performance numbers are strong but peer or manager feedback highlights serious concerns (such as poor collaboration, recurring conflicts, or resistance to feedback), the promotion must be paused. High output does not override behavioral risks.
In such cases, HR must clearly document the contradiction and define specific improvements required before reconsideration.

Avoiding Bias in Promotion Decisions

Bias must be actively avoided at all stages. Decisions must not be influenced by gender, age, personality style, personal closeness, or recent events alone. Language such as “too emotional,” “aggressive,” “not confident enough,” or vague praise without evidence must not be used. All feedback must be supported by specific examples.

HR’s Role in Ensuring Fairness

HR is responsible for:

Reviewing all promotion cases for consistency

Ensuring similar performance levels receive similar outcomes

Questioning unclear or unsupported recommendations

Confirming that both results and behavior are evaluated

Maintaining written justification for every decision

Promotions are approved only when performance, behavior, and fairness standards are clearly met.
